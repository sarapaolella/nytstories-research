Abstract

This research employs computational corpus linguistics methodologies to investigate the impact of the Covid-19 pandemic on semantic diversity and lexical variability within contemporary short fiction. Through systematic analysis of reader-submitted narratives published in The New York Times "Tiny Love Stories" column during the critical first year of the global health emergency (February 2020â€“February 2021), this study identifies measurable patterns of lexical convergence and thematic repetition directly attributable to collective pandemic experience.

Research Question

"How did the Covid-19 pandemic transform the language, thematic content, and semantic variability of short-form love narratives published during the first year of the global emergency?"

Corpus Linguistics: Foundational Concepts

A corpus constitutes "a collection of pieces of language that are selected and ordered according to explicit linguistic criteria..." (Sinclair, 1996).

Tokens, Types, and Lexical Variability

Tokens represent the total number of words. Types denote distinct lexical items. The Token-Type Ratio (TTR) provides a quantitative measure of lexical variability.

Collocational Analysis

Collocates are words that co-occur with statistically significant frequency within proximate textual positions.
